Narrative

After many years of digging around for my Ellenberger and Harshberger/Herschberger lines, it is my understanding that they were originally Swiss Mennonites, not German (though German-speakers, as are most Swiss today).
Some people w/these surnames were severely punished for being Anabaptists in Switzerland during the Reformation. They were persecuted not only by the Catholic Church, but also by the new Protestant Lutherans for preaching against infant baptism. There are records of Herschbergers having tongues cut out and fingers cut off because they would not stay out of the cantons once banned for preaching against infant baptism. Likewise, there are records of these Mennonite (Anabaptists) being burned alive in their barns while holding religious meetings. I think there is an ancient book written with these records (Something about a "mirror")used as a sort of history book w/young Mennonites who fled to PA in the early 1700's to explain their beliefs, martyrdom, and why they had to flee Europe and set up "shop" in Pennsylvania. Many of these 2 surnames fled into the Palitinate of (now) Germany and parts of (now---German-speaking) France, and then to Pennsylvania.
In particular, the earliest Ellenberger I find in my line is a Niggli Ellenberger b c.1634 in Switzerland, near Ruderswil. He m. Christina Neuhaus. Their son, Nicholas b. 20Jan1667 Ruderswil had 3 children, the first being Nicholas b 1698 in the Palitinate of (now) Germany. So, you can see the approx. time they fled (were banned from) Switzerland.
It was this Nicholas Ellenberger in my family, born in "exile" in the Palitinate,who came to PA, where he d. 1760 Annville Twp.Lebanon Co He arrived 3 Sept 1739 in PA on ship "Robert&Alice". His son, Jacob b.1735/6 m 1st Anna Huntskaker, nxt. Feronica ?. His son John b 1768 m. Margaret. This John had 2 sons John b 1802 and Jacob b 1793, who moved to Centre Co. PA.
I descend fr. the Centre Co. Jacob b 1793 who m. Elizabeth Rumberger---the Rumbergers also, I believe, being Swiss Mennonites. These last are buried at Ross Methodist Church in Marengo, Ferguson Twp, Centre Co PA, along with many descendants. They lived mainly in the Gatesburg/Stormstown/Port Matilda area through descendant generations.
