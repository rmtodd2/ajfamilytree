Narrative

This info was given to us by Susan (Hodges) Todd

Dad doesn't know where Benjamin Sine was born. He was adopted by a family named French early in his life. Mary has a picture of him with the French's in her living room. His first love was engineering and I know he moved his family out to California to work along that line with his brother -- but his wife begged him to return East so he moved the family back to NY State. He had a dairy farm in Meridian, New York and went to Cato every day including Sunday to deliver his milk to the train station to the cities for processing -- a bunch of farmers in the area did that. He had a lot of machinery -- 4 gasoline engines including one for milking, one for thrashing, and also a water pump. Also had a 1919 or 1920 Studebaker as well as a big Ford truck with a flat bed, the kind with the fence around it. He had a big farmhouse which had a separate wing for a hired man and his family to live in. Besides the cattle he grew all the crops to feed them (corn, wheat, barley) and had a garden for the family.

 

His son Harold's wife was named Geneva -- they had a son David Sine. After they divorced she took care of her uncle Frank. Harold was a bootlegger bringing boose across from Canada during Prohibition.

 

His daughter Marjorie married Avon Cromartie who was a chiropracter. They had three girls: Joy, Elizabeth, and Jean. Elizabeth married a Derrenbacher and lived in North Carolina, not sure when.

Daughter Mildred married a Lawrence Leigh who was a Physical Education teacher. They had a son Donald Leigh.
