Narrative

Veteran Employe Of Plow Company Dies at His Home.
Athanasius Maier to be Buried Wednesday.

Athanasius Maier, employe of John Deere Plow company 47 years and president of German Pioneer society, died at 1 o'clock yesterday afternoon at his home, 111 Steuben street. He was 68 years old. Born in Renquishausen, Germany, he came to this country 50 years ago and entered the employ of the plow company. He retired three years ago. He was a member of Schwaben Verein, Catholic federation; Court Salt City, Foresters of America; and a communicant of the Church of the Assumption. Surviving are his wife, Mrs. Brigitta Stengele Maier; three daughters, Sister Hilda of the Franciscan order in Syracuse; Sister Rosemary of Missionary Sisters of Blessed Sisters in Philadelphia, Pa.; and Miss Mamie Maier of Syracuse; four sons, Joseph, Bert, Edwin and Frank Maier; a brother, Stanislaus Maier of Germany; and seven grandchildren. Services will be held at the home at 9.30 o'clock Wednesday morning and in the Church of the Assumption at 10 o'clock. Burial will be in Assumption cemetery.

(from: Syracuse Post Standard Monday 29.08.1932 pg 7 col 6)

Athanasius MAIER / MAYER, son of Athanasius MAIER and Anna Mueller, b. abt. May 1864, Renquishausen, Wuertemberg, Germany; died 28 August 1932, Syracuse, Onondaga County, New York. He married, first, in Germany (wife's name unknown) before 1880, and had a daughter, Joseph MAIER, b. abt. 1880, Kolbingen, Wuertemberg, Germany, who became a Sister of St. Francis in Syracuse and died there 27 August 1968. Athanasius emigrated with his siblings to the United States in 1883 and settled in Syracuse.

He married, second, Anna M. SCHMIDT, daughter of Jacob SCHMIDT and Anna WIELAND, 12 November 1885, Church of the Assumption, Syracuse. Witnesses were John Schmidt and Maria Herder. They apparently had no children.

She died before 1889 when Athanasius married, third, as her second husband, Birgitta STENGELE, widow of Anselm MAIER (1854-1887), 6 May 1889 in the Church of the Assumption. A dispensation from the second degree of affinity was obtained. By her he had: Anna, b. abt. Sept. 1889, Anna, who became Sr. Rosemary, M.S.B.T.; Mary A. (1890-1986); Joseph Aloysius (1892- ); Engelbert A. "Bert" (1894-1974); Herman J. (1895-1897); Edwin H. (1897- ); and Frank Herman / Frank J. (1898-1953. They were all born in Syracuse.

Anselm MAIER (1854-1887) and Birgitta STENGELE had Maria, who died 7 May 1883, Syracuse; Joseph (1881-1886); Frank E. (1884-1886).
